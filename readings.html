As We May Think

As We May Think (reading reflection):

In As We May Think, Vannevar Bush addresses the application of science to inventions that reproduce, replace, improve humans’ minds and knowledge rather than focusing on increasing their physical powers. For a long time, scientists such as physicists or engineers used their knowledge to find ways to better people’s lives and material environments by designing and creating devices, mechanisms, gadgets that would reproduce our physical abilities sometimes at a much higher level to help us in our daily lives. However, after World War II, Bush reflected on how would our lives be improved if we could use these technologies to the conception of devices that would store people’s knowledge?

The human mind works in a very particular way. The chain of thoughts in our brain works with association. An idea leads to another depending on their similarities transmitted extremely rapidly via brain cells, an operation that is especially difficult to reproduce technologically. Accordingly, in 1945, to express his idea, Bush introduced a theoretical, futuristic invention: memex. The goal of this device was to collect a great number of memories, records, “books”, just like a library. This storage of knowledge in what Bush imagined as a “desk” would work with association at a significant speed and fluidity similar to the human brain.

For his hypothetical creation, Bush was inspired by the already known bodily mechanisms. Personally, I have always been interested in the human body and bodily functions and in finding inspiration in it which is why I find Bush’s idea so interesting. Finding his inspiration in how our brain functions was very smart. I think that he was well ahead of his time regarding the storage of knowledge in an object. Moreover, as Bush mentions it, the human brain isn’t perfect. If certain brain cells are not stimulated and used enough, the memory linked to this trail might vanish. I believe that storing our knowledge into a device such as memex would have avoided that while accumulating even more knowledge that our brain can. Thinking of such a machine that would exceed our human brain’s limitations in 1945 was impressing.

Marie Taoko

Virtual Revolution: The great Levelling (reflection):

In Virtual Revolution – The great Levelling, Dr. Aleks Krotoski introduces the history of the internet and the world wide web, and their actions and repercussions on our lives, our world, our society.

As presented in the documentary, the internet which began in the second half of the 20th century was a virtual revolution. It was an empowering tool for all, a way to have access to knowledge, a way for “ordinary people to create something extraordinary”, a way to shake knowledge together. The internet is described as a great “legacy of counterculture”, that can encapsulate peoples’ values, fights, demonstrations, idealisms… It is a way for people to fight real life struggles online, struggles that then take whole new dimensions. The internet revolutionized communication and the storing of knowledge and as figures such as John Barlow mentions it, the internet became a challenge to traditional authority. Indeed, it was a free place for self-expression that had no boundaries, people became able to express themselves, their ideas, their voices.

It is in 1989 that Tim Burners-Lee made his first proposal of what he call “Information Management” that soon became the World Wide Web, and in 1991, the first website was posted online. This created a whole new line of information for all people with a computer. Figures like Bill Gates and Steve Wozniak explained their relation to the internet and the web and expressed how it quickly became a vast business. People were able to share information with everyone all around the world, being connected empowered people who could learn and find everything online on their own. It allowed everyone to publish whatever they wanted in any format with no controlling authority. Thus, quickly arrived illegal sharing of information, music, videos. Today everyone can access so many sources thanks to YouTube or Social Medias such as Facebook. People can upload whatever they want.

Personally, I believe that the internet and the web are great inventions that greatly better and facilitate our lives. We can now find so much information so easily which makes learning and communication faster and more effective. Nowadays, a lot of people, companies, organizations use the web daily. It is a platform so practical and instinctive that even young children learn to use it very quickly and easily. I think that the main danger and issue of the internet is how some people use it. Examples of cyber bullying or raising hate come to mind. Freedom of speech and the fact that the internet is almost uncontrollable have allowed people to post all sorts of hurtful ideas. Even though I am for freedom and being able to express ourselves to a certain point, I believe that the internet can be dangerous because as it is said in the documentary, everyone can use it and share everything with no restrictions. That said, I still believe that the web is a great part of our lives now and that it has improved our ways of working, communicating, advertising, entertaining, and more

Marie Taoko
